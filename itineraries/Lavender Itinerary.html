kallang gasworks

The predecessor to today's Public Utilities Board supplying gas to the public. It was initially named Huay Sia, Hokkien for "fire city", because of superstitious fears it would explode. As Singapore modernised, demand for gas was reduced and it was less relied on.

directions

From Lavender MRT Station, leave through Exit A and turn right onto Kallang Road. Walk against the direction of the traffic on your side. When the path starts to bend, cross the road to the other side of Crawford Street. Turn right and walk in the direction of the traffic. Keep walking until you see a sign that reads ‘Kallang Riverside Park’. Shortly after that, you should see a white bridge, Rochor Link. Cross the bridge. Turn right, and then follow the path. Watch out for Gas Holder No. 3 on your left. You can cross the road to take a better look. If, after you cross the bridge, you follow the path to your left, you will reach theSpirit of Kallang sculpture, a part of Kallang Gasworks.

Use the camera to capture the plaque found on one of the pillars of Gasholder No. 3, a structure in tribute to the demolished Kallang Gasworks, the Spirit of Kallang sculpture or the ‘Kallang Riverside Park’ sign for more information.
