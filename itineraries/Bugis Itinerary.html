sultan mosque

Widely considered Singapore's national mosque as it was constructed on a personal request from Sultan Hussein to Raffles. When it was renovated, while wealthier Muslims contributed funds, the poor donated glass bottles ends to construct the base of its 2 minarets. 

directions

From Bugis MRT Station, leave through Exit B. Walk along Victoria Street past Raffles Hospital and cross Ophir Road. Walk past Golden Landmark Shopping Complex and turn right into Arab Street. Cross North Bridge Road. Turn left to the Sultan Mosque.

Use the camera to capture the golden dome for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0930 - 1200 and 1400 - 1600

*Closed on Fridays

malay heritage centre

A cultural centre sharing the historical significance of the Malays and Kampong Glam as a thriving port town. At the Dagang gallery, learn about the maritime nature of Singapore's early Malays before appreciating the literary and musical traditions of the Malay community in the Budaya gallery.

directions

Cross Arab Street. You should be on the same side of the street as the mosque. Walk down Arab Street in the direction of traffic on your side and turn left into Muscat Street. Pass an archway and walk into the mosque area, down Muscat Street. Keep walking straight down until you reach another archway. Directly across you would be the Malay Heritage Centre.

Use the camera to capture the entrance of the building for more information.

admission 

Ticket prices: $ 2 - 4

Operating hours: 0800 - 2000

*Closed on Mondays

bugis village

A tight enclave of 34 uniquely restored pre - war shop houses, each 3 storeys high, that houses businesses and retail shops. It used to hawk traditional medicine such as bat meat and beef tongue, an aphrodisiac for men.

directions

Walk along Kandahar Street against the direction of traffic on your side and reach North Bridge Road. Turn left and walk in the direction of the traffic until you reach Arab Street. Cross to the other side of North Bridge Road, and continue to walk along Arab Street, in the direction of traffic.

Once you reach Victoria Street, cross the road to other side and continue walking against the direction of traffic on your side. You will cross Ophir Road and Rochor Road. Before crossing Rochor Road, use the camera to capture the Bugis Village sign with an advertisement board below for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 1100 - 2200

kwan im thong hood cho temple

A Buddhist temple most visited on the 1st and 15th days of the Chinese New Year, when devout worshippers offer incense in hopes of an auspicious start to the year. The incense urn is placed outside the temple hall to prevent soot from staining the ceiling.

directions

Walk along Victoria Street against the direction of traffic on your side. Turn right into Cheng Yan Place and walk until you reach Queen Street. Cross Queen Street. Turn right and walk along Queen Street in the direction of the traffic. Turn left into Bencoolen Link, turn left again, at the first intersection, into Waterloo Street, and walk to Kwan Im Temple.

Use the camera to capture the temple entrance for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0600 - 1800
