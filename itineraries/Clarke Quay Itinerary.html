old thong chai medical institution

A rare surviving example of Southern Chinese secular architecture, incorporating vividly - painted scenery reliefs and carved timber panels. It symbolises the spirit of mutual assistance amongst early Chinese settlers, when doctors dispensed free treatment and medicine to patients of all races. 

directions

From Clarke Quay MRT Station, leave through Exit B and turn left. Cross Merchant Road to Old Thong Chai Medical Institution.

Use the camera to capture the building or the Chinese and English signs at its entrance for more information.

masjid omar kampong melaka

Singapore's 1st mosque that served as a gathering place for both the local Muslim community and the continuous flow of Arabs and Indonesians. Despite being located in the middle of a large Chinese community, the mosque remained unharmed during the Prophet Mohammed racial riots of 1964.

directions

Turn right and walk along Merchant Road. Cross New Market Road past Park Regis Singapore and turn left into Keng Cheow Street, and walk a small distance.

Use the camera to capture the blue sign at the entrance, the golden minaret or the silver board for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0830 - 1630 [Weekend] and 0500 - 2300 [Weekday]

mica building

A colonial landmark recognisable by its multi - coloured window shutters, also known as the Old Hill Street Police Station for having housed the police headquarters in the mid - 20th century. It was later occupied by the National Archives and finally MICA.

directions

Turn left and walk back along Keng Cheow Street. Turn right and walk along Merchant Road past Park Regis Singapore.

Cross New Market Street and walk past Old Thong Chai Medical Institution. Turn left and cross Merchant Road.

Walk along Eu Tong Sen Street in the direction of the traffic past The Central and Singapore River. Cross River Valley Road to the MICA Building.

Use the camera to capture the signboard to your left, which has a history of the building or the wooden statues for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 1000 - 1700

central fire station

The oldest fire station in Singapore, symbolic of the establishment of a modern fire - fighting system from the original volunteer fire service. During the Japanese Occupation, the Central Fire Station and the British firemen were retained and hence spared incarceration.

directions

Turn left and walk along Hill Street in the direction of the traffic to Central Fire Station. Use the camera to capture the building, the entrance sign, plaque, statues outside the building and wooden board displaying the opening hours for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 1000 - 1700
