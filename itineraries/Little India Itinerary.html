sri veeramakaliamman temple

One of the oldest Hindu temples in Singapore dedicated to Kali, the goddess of power. This was built by early Indian immigrants to recreate a familiar aspect of their homeland, and the choice of Kali was to obtain a sense of security in a foreign land.

directions

From Little India MRT Station, leave through Exit E and walk down Buffalo Road. Turn left at Serangoon Road and walk past Kerbau Road and Belilios Road to Sri Veeramakaliamman Temple.

Use the camera to capture the temple front, its side address or its left gate for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0800 - 1230 and 1600 - 2030

little india arcade

A shopping area selling authentic Indian food [betel leaves, curry], music [Bollywood tracks] and fashion [traditional sarees and dhotis]. Its cluster of shop house buildings dates back from the early 20th century while its winding alleys house bookshops, curio and sweet shops.

directions

Turn left and walk back along Serangoon Road past Kerbau Road and Buffalo Road. Cross Serangoon Road and turn right. Walk past Campbell Lane to reach Little India Arcade.

Use the camera to capture the overhead sign for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0900 - 2200

masjid abdul gaffoor mosque

A mosque known for its unusual symmetry, layout and incorporation of Moorish, Saracenic and Roman designs. Besides conventional Islamic details, many Western classical motifs, including Corinthian [Grecian] style columns, are also used in decorations. 

directions

Turn left and walk along Serangoon Road against the direction of traffic on your side past Hastings Road. Turn in at Sungei Road and walk past The Verge. Turn left into Perak Street and turn right into Mayo Street.

Use the camera to capture Masjid Abdul Gaffoor Mosque for more information.

Free

Operating hours: 0800 - 2400
