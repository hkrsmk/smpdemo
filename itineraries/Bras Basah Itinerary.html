singapore art museum

Singapore's national contemporary art centre that houses the world's largest public collection of modern Southeast Asian works. It traces its roots to the National Museum, which embarked on a project to set up a 5 - museum precinct in Singapore. 

directions

From Bras Basah MRT Station, leave through Exit A. Cross Queen Street to the Singapore Art Museum. Use the camera to capture the statue at the alcove entrance for more information.

admission 

Ticket prices: $ 5 - 10

Operating hours: 1000 - 1900

cathedral of the good shepherd

Singapore's oldest Roman Catholic church, constructed in the likeness of London's St. Paul's and St. Martin - in - the - Fields, and named in honour of the 1st priest to visit Singapore, French missionary St. Imbert, who wrote "In desperate circumstances, the good shepherd lays down his life for his sheep".

directions

Cross Queen Street and turn right. Walk past NTUC Trade Union House and cross Bras Basah Road to Cathedral of the Good Shepherd.

Use the camera to capture the entrance sign of the church's opening hours for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0700 - 1800

peranakan museum

Complements the Asian Civilisations Museum and explores Peranakan culture in Southeast Asia. Look for the Peranakan wedding bed that  belonged to a Panang Peranakan where she gave birth to 7 children.

directions

Turn left and walk along Bras Basah Road. Turn left and walk along Victoria Street past the SMRT Headquarters. Cross Stamford Road and walk past Stamford House.

Turn right and cross Victoria Street into Loke Yew Street. Cross Armenian Street to the Peranakan Museum.

Use the camera to capture the entrance of the museum of the 2 statues for more information.

admission 

Ticket prices: $ 3 - 6

Operating hours: 1000 - 1900

national museum of singapore

Singapore's oldest museum. Its humble roots lead back to a mere section of a library at Singapore Institution, today's Raffles Institution, a premier school for boys. During the Japanese Occupation, it was left intact by the Japanese army due to its longstanding reputation of major collections and research integrity. 

directions

Turn left and walk along Armenian Street past shop houses. Cross the traffic junction at Fort Canning Link and turn left.

Walk past the School of Law and Accountancy to the National Museum of Singapore. Use the camera to capture the stone structures at the entrance of the museum for more information.

admission 

Ticket prices: $ 5 - 10

Operating hours: 1000 - 1800

fort canning park

A small hill with the highest elevation within walking distance to the civic district. It was home to Singapore's 1st botanical garden and governor residence before it was converted into a lookout centre to watch for intruding forces.

directions

From the back entrance of National Museum of Singapore, cross the zebra crossing at Fort Canning Link to Fort Canning Park.

Use the camera to capture the wooden sign of the park for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0000 - 2400
