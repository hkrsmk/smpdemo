asian civilisations museum

One of Singapore's 3 National Museums which specialises in Asian artefacts as the roots of local history and culture. The Chinese collection displays Dehua porcelain figures and religious statuary while the South Asian gallery features Chola bronzes, including the noted Uma sculpture.

directions

From Raffles Place MRT Station, leave through Exit H and walk along Battery Road past the Bank of China. Turn left after Maybank Towers and cross the Cavenagh Bridge.

You should see a statue of children jumping into the Singapore River on its right, and at the end of the bridge, there should be a 1910s police notice.

Walk straight to see the Asian Civilisations Museum.

Use the camera to capture the front facade of the museum, especially the sign at the top, for more information.

admission 

Ticket prices: $ 2 - 8

Operating hours: 1000 - 1900 [Daily] and 1000 - 2100 [Friday]

merlion park

2 Merlion [the national personification of Singapore] structures that spout water. The original, larger statue is 8.6 m tall and stands amid the city skyline, aligned to face the auspicious East direction.

directions

Walk past Fullerton Hotel, the statue of children jumping into the Singapore River and the cruise ticketing office to exit at Fullerton Hotel. Turn right and cross Fullerton Road.

Walk down the staircase. Turn right and walk straight.

Use the camera to capture the small Merlion statue for more information.

lau pa sat festival market

A large food court combining traditional culinary styles and architecture with the comfort of an alfresco dining experience. Distinctly local flavours include Dum Briyani from Andhra Curry [Stall 3], Ban Mian from Qiu Lian [Stall 83] and Filipino food from Panyero [Stall 31]. 

directions

Walk back to Fullerton Road. Walk past the HSBC Building in Fullerton Square, and return to the Raffles Place MRT Station.

From the Raffles Place MRT Station, leave through Exit I, walking past the AIA Tower and the Hong Leong Building, both located at Robinson Road, to get to Lau Pa Sat Festival Market.

Use the camera to capture the signboard at the entrance of the market (with the words "A taste of Singapore's past served fresh") for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0000 - 2400

nagore dargah heritage centre

A shrine built by Muslims from Southern India. Its atypical facade resembles a multi - tiered wedding cake with sharp arches unusual of classical motifs and perforated grilles at the roof, an Indian Muslim element.

directions

Cross Robinson Road and Market Road. Walk straight and cross Cecil Street.

Walk along Cross Street. You will see signs directing you to Nagore Dargah Heritage Shrine. Follow the signs and cross Boon Tat Street.

Use the camera to capture any of the 2 signboards on the gates of the Nagore Dargah Heritage Centre for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 1000 - 1400 [Weekend] and 1000 - 1800 [Weekday]

*Closed on Sundays and public holidays

thian hock keng temple

Singapore's oldest Hokkien temple dedicated to Ma Zu, goddess of the sea who protected Chinese immigrants during their voyages and Guanyin, the bodhisattva of mercy. Guarding its doors are tigers, lions and Door Gods, traditional guards of Taoist temples.

directions

Walk along Telok Ayer Street and use the camera to capture the yellow sign at the entrance of Thian Hock Keng Temple for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0730 - 1730

al - abrar mosque

A mosque built for Tamil Muslims, Singapore's earliest immigrants who lived and worked in the Telok Ayer area. It is aligned towards Mecca, and has a relatively simple design of a one - storey prayer hall with 4 undecorated minarets.

directions

Walk along Telok Ayer Street past the shop houses and use the camera to capture the sign at the entrance of the mosque for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 1100 - 1900 [Weekday]

*Closed on weekends
