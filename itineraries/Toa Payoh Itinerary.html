shuang lin monastery

Singapore's 1st Buddhist monastery that symbolised the successful migrants' perceived obligations towards society as the building of a traditional monastery in a foreign land. It is modelled after Xi Chan Si, a famed monastery in China where the 1st abbot studied in.

directions

From Toa Payoh MRT Station Exit B, turn left and walk to take bus 235 or 238 and alight 3 stops later at Block 32 [ striped orange and white building]. Cross the overhead bridge at the bus stop.

Walk down Lorong 6 Toa Payoh to Kim Keat Link in the direction of the traffic on your side. Look out for signs that point you towards the Monastery. Turn left into Blocks 25-27, 194, 195 and 241 - 260, and walk along the road until you reach another road. Turn right and walk down the road. You should see the Monastery.

Use the camera to capture the main entrance, the first sign you see with the Monastery name, or the sign on the main gate for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 0830 - 1700

sun yat sen nanyang memorial hall

A 2 - storey museum commemorating Sun Yat Sen, a Chinese revolutionary who is recognised as the founder of the Republic of China and a uniting figure in post - imperial China. This villa served as the Tongmenghui's Southeast Asian base where 3 uprisings leading up to the Xinhai Revolution were planned.

directions

At the roundabout, look out for an overhead bridge and climb onto it. You'll have to walk along the road a bit to get to an opening so that you can get onto the bridge.

After crossing the bridge and going down the stairs, look out for another flight of stairs to your right. Climb down, and walk into the carpark between and the row of private houses on your left. Cross Jalan Rajah at Block 107. Turn right and walk along Jalan Rajah in the direction of the traffic on your side. Walk past Curtin Education Centre to the next bus stop. Take bus 139 and alight 2 stops later opposite East Asia Institute of Management.

Turn right, walk against the direction of traffic on your side and cross the road at the zebra crossing. Continue walking along Ah Hood Road in the direction of the traffic on your side. Walk past Pentecostal Church to the Sun Yat Sen Nanyang Memorial Hall.

Use your camera to capture the main signage or the statue of Sun Yat Sen within the building for more information.

admission 

Ticket price: $2 - 4

Operating hours: 1000 - 1700
