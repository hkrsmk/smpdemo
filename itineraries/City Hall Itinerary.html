st. andrew's cathedral

Singapore's 1st and largest Anglican church whose stained glass windows are dedicated to the founder of Singapore, Stamford Raffles, 2nd Resident Crawfurd and Major General Butterworth. During World War II, it was used as an emergency hospital for casualties of frequent bombings. 

directions

From City Hall MRT Station, leave through Exit B and turn left along North Bridge Road to St. Andrew's Cathedral. Use the camera to capture the entrance of the church or the signboard at the bus stop for more information.

old parliament house

The existing holding site for the Parliament and the Supreme Court that was converted into a multi - disciplinary arts venue hosting exhibitions and concerts. It is the oldest surviving government building in Singapore.

directions

Turn left along North Bridge Road and cross Coleman Street. Walk past The Adelphi and the Supreme Court. Cross Parliament Place and turn left. Walk past Parliament House to the Old Parliament House. Use the camera to capture the entrance logo or the elephant statue for more information.

admission 

Free

Operating hours: 1000 - 2000

raffles landing site

The supposed location where Stamford Raffles first landed on, after having remained aboard his ship Indiana till locals called on board. The present statue was made from plaster casts from the original figure that stands opposite Victoria Concert Hall. 

directions

From the back entrance of Old Parliament House, walk along Empress Place past Asian Civilisations Museum to Raffles Landing Site.

Use the camera to capture the English plaque of the statue for more information.
